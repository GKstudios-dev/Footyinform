Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 2026

Welcome to The Football Post. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle your information when you use our app and website.

Key Point: Reading news on The Football Post never requires an account, and we collect nothing from you to do that. Creating a free account (only needed to like articles or leave comments) collects an email address and a username you choose — we do not collect, see, or store your password.

1. Information We Collect

You can read every article on The Football Post — club news, national team news, transfers, and breaking news — without creating an account or providing any personal information.

If you choose to create an account (only required to like an article or leave a comment), we collect:

  • Email address — used to sign you in. This is handled by Firebase Authentication (a Google service); we never see or store your password ourselves.
  • Username — the display name you choose at sign-up. This is what appears next to any comment you post, instead of your email.

If you like an article or post a comment, we also store:

  • Which articles you've liked, linked to your account, so we can show your like back to you and prevent duplicate likes.
  • The text of any comment you post, along with your username and the time it was posted.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your email address solely to sign you in and secure your account. We use your username to identify your comments to other readers. We use your likes and comments only to display them back within the app and website — we do not use this information for advertising profiling, and we do not sell it to anyone.

3. Passwords

We do not collect or store your password. Account sign-in is handled entirely by Firebase Authentication (a Google service), which stores passwords in encrypted form on Google's infrastructure — The Football Post itself never has access to your password in readable form.

4. Data Storage

We use Firebase (a Google service) as our cloud backend — specifically Firebase Authentication for account sign-in, and Cloud Firestore to store articles, your username, your likes, and your comments. Firebase may also collect anonymous usage data for analytics, which is governed by Google's own privacy policies.

5. Comments Are Public

Any comment you post is visible to every reader of that article, shown alongside the username you chose. Please don't include personal information you wouldn't want made public in a comment. You can delete your own comments at any time; we (the app owner) can also remove comments that violate our Terms of Use.

6. Third-Party Services

Our app and website use the following third-party services:

  • Firebase Authentication (by Google) – for account sign-in
  • Cloud Firestore (by Google) – for storing articles, usernames, likes, and comments

These services have their own privacy policies, which you can review on Google's website.

7. Your Rights

You can delete any comment you've posted at any time from within the app or website. If you'd like your account and associated data (email, username, likes, comments) deleted entirely, contact us using the details below and we'll process that request.

8. Children's Privacy

The Football Post is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect information from children under 13. If you believe a child has created an account, please contact us so we can remove it.

9. Future Updates

If we add features that change what data we collect (for example, showing real advertisements), we will update this Privacy Policy and note the change here.
